Highlights of Recent Database Upgrades

Eighth Database Release (December, 2010):
  • Information on 132 new genes and 292 new mutants included in the database.
  • Links to archival information on embryo mutants from the Meinke laboratory.
  • Links to additional project publications and meeting abstracts.
  • Link to Supplemental Gene Dataset for future additions to the database.
  • Link to Supplemental Alias Symbols obtained from recent publications.
  • Notification of future links to additional datasets of genes with mutant phenotypes.
  • Nine mutants with questionable gene identities removed from the database:
    • EMB 1745 (At1g13120)
    • EMB 2778 (At4g11820)
    • EMB 1353 (At4g30090)
    • EMB 1879 (At5g49820)
    • EMB 2780 (At5g63960)
    • EMB 3001 (At1g08910)
    • EMB 1011 (At1g55540)
    • EMB 2301 (At2g46770)
    • EMB 3022 (At4g17810)
Seventh Database Release (December, 2007):
  • Information on 70 new genes and 150 new mutants included in the database.
  • Another 46 genes included in the Pending Additions link on the Access page.
  • New list of 62 genes (31 pairs) that give a double mutant seed phenotype.
  • New list of 1,412 embryo-defective mutants recently contributed to ABRC.
  • Option to download Excel spreadsheet versions of all mutants and genes.
  • Updated information on mutant phenotypes and predicted gene functions.
  • Updated sequence and BLAST information consistent with TAIR 7.0.
  • Direct links to Nomarski image montages from compiled lists of mutants.
Prior Changes in Database and Website Design
  • Access to SeedGenes no longer requires registration and log-in.
  • Direct links to internal pages can therefore be generated in the future.
  • Database upgraded from Oracle to MySQL to facilitate future development.
Sixth Database Release (September, 2004):
  • Information on 34 new genes and 48 new mutants included in the database.
  • A tutorial with detailed instructions on analysis of embryo-defective mutants.
  • Mutants and gene identities contributed by INRA/Genoplante collaborators.
  • Improved visualization of flanking sequence details for insertion mutants.
  • Updated gene information based on TIGR 5.0 and TAIR (November, 2004).
  • Additional classification system based on the confidence of gene identity.
  • Ability to query the database with lists of genes and find those included.
  • Automated BLAST results based on updated GenBank and TIGR information.
  • Links to seed stocks at the Arabidopsis Biological Resource Center (ABRC).
  • Additional Nomarski images of cleared seeds from embryo-defective mutants.
  • Updated lists of SeedGenes personnel and links to project publications.
  • Descriptions of outreach activities associated with the SeedGenes project.
